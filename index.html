The Different Colors of the Moon

Why does the Moon look blue one night and red another? Let’s examine the most common Moon colors and the things that cause them to appear.

❤️❤️❤️
Pink Moon

The term "Pink Moon" refers to the full moon that appears in April. It's named after pink wildflowers called "phlox" that bloom around that time in some areas. The name doesn't indicate the moon's color; rather, it's a traditional name tied to nature's cycles.

Red Moon

Blood moons" occur during total lunar eclipses when Earth's atmosphere scatters shorter wavelengths of light, leaving only red hues to reach and illuminate the Moon.

Blue Moon

Blue moons are rare occurrences tied to dusty or smoky atmospheres, often seen after volcanic eruptions like Krakatoa in 1883.

Full Moon

A full moon happens when the entire Moon is lit due to its position opposite the Sun. This phase lasts around three days before the Moon's orbit shifts, altering our perspective.

The Moon: Earth's Singular Natural Satellite

The Moon is our closest neighbor in the galaxy, orbiting Earth just 238,855 miles (384,400 kilometers) away. Earth has only this one natural satellite, unlike other planets in our solar system, most of which have several moons.
